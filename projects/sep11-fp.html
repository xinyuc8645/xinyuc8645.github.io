For Part A the requirement was to research an software that would relate to topic of animation. It didn't take me long to find one. And the one I found was adobe. Upon researching, adobe I learned that adobe has so many tools an animator need. And for the Part B, we were required to research an hardware. We had to search 3 more specifically. The 3 I found that relate to my topic of animation were, CPU, drawing tablet, and zoetrope. If you don't know what an zoetrope is, basically it is an old animating tool, where you have certain frames of an object, then you put that frame in the zoetrope in an circle, then it would start spinning very fast, making it seem like if that object was actually moving. And as for the other 2 it's pretty self explanatory. And that's basically all for Part A and Part B. Oh, and before I forget, part B required an future invention. To be honest I kind of forgotten about this.

So, I had to quickly think up of one. And the future invention I came up with is an robotic tool/hand that would help reduce the animators time needed to animate an certain frame, so their hand doesn't get tire and would require less work and would result in more faster production. Challenges

To be honest, there wasn't any too difficult challenge I faced for this project, but if I had to say one it probably had to do with paragraphs not aligning and images proportions. The reason why I am saynig this is because, if you look at my website for the section Part A and Part B, you notice there is 3 paragraphs side by slide. However, prior to this happening it wasn't side by side, instead it was constantly overlapping each other. I kept trying to put it in container-fluid, rows, and etc. However that didn't really help out. Instead it reminded me of something. At the start of the bootstrap lesson, I learned that an basic screen size there is 12 grids spreaded evenly across. So that means if I have 3 paragraphs and wanted them to be side by side, that would mean I just have to make them take up 4 space each paragraph, and so I did. I applied that to both Part A and Part B. Honestly this was a life saver, because if I didn't learn about bootstrap or think of this, honestly I would of showcased my website, with overlapping paragraphs instead.

Another challenge I face was with the image proportion specifically for my carousel. The reason is because when I applied the carousel on to my website, and put the images, when I refreshed the page, the image were so large that it basically takes up 100% of the screen. At first I didn't know what to do. But then I turned to width and height. I changed both height and width but that didn't seemed to work. So instead of changing both, I changed only width to 85% so that it wouldn't take up 100% of the website size.
